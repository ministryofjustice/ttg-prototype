How to get proof of ID

To get benefits, or apply for a job, you’ll need photographic evidence to prove who you are, such as:

  • UK passport
  • EEA passport
  • Residence permit or card
  • Immigration status document
  • Registration or naturalisation certificate
  • UK photo driving licence
  • National identity card

Full list of DWP acceptable documents[PDF]

If you have no photographic ID

You can apply for a National identity card (CitizenCard) or a provisional driving licence.

CitizenCard

CitizenCard is an official UK ID card that you can use to prove who you are when you apply for benefits. It costs £7.50 if you use your probation officer as a referee.

They'll need to register with CitizenCard and countersign a photo of you. Ask them to contact CitizenCard.

You can apply online yourself, but this will cost £15. You'll still need someone to act as a referee.

Apply for CitizenCard online

Provisional driving licence

A provisional driving licence can also be used to prove who you are. It costs £43.

Apply for provisional driving licence

Other evidence you’ll need

To get benefits, you can use form B79 that was given to you on release. This shows you’ve been discharged from prison and have been advised to claim benefits.

You’ll also need another document to prove things you state, such as:

  • tenancy agreement
  • up-to-date rent statement
  • wage slip
  • bank statement
  • proof of savings

Full list of DWP acceptable documents[PDF]

When you need ID you'll often be asked for one item of 'primary' (photographic) evidence and two items of 'secondary' evidence so it's important to have access to these documents.
