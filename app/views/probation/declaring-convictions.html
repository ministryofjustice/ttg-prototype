Disclosing your convictions

What you have to disclose depends on whether your convictions are spent or unspent.

Spent convictions

For most jobs, you don’t have to disclose them, even if you’re asked to.

They won’t be disclosed on a basic criminal record check.

However, some jobs involve a standard or enhanced criminal record check. For these jobs, you have to disclose spent convictions.

You don’t have to disclose them when buying insurance.

You might have to disclose them when travelling or working outside of England and Wales.

Spent convictions remain on your record for life.

Unspent convictions

If asked by an employer, you must disclose them.

You must disclose them for insurance, mortgages, renting a house and so on.

You can be prosecuted if you don’t disclose them when asked.
